# 0. CORE RESEARCH DIRECTION — DO NOT CHANGE THIS TOPIC

The research topic is fixed:

## Proposed strongest topic

**Latent World Models for Video Game Glitch Detection: A JEPA-based Approach**

## Dataset direction

Primary datasets may include:

* VideoGlitch / VideoGlitchBench
* GlitchBench
* World of Bugs
* TempGlitch
* Atari / ALE / Procgen only as supporting dynamics benchmarks, not as the main topic unless necessary

## Research hypothesis

Video game bugs/glitches create abnormal deviations in latent dynamics compared with normal gameplay. A latent world model trained or adapted on normal gameplay can detect glitches by measuring latent prediction error / surprise score.

## Method direction

Train or fine-tune LeWM on normal gameplay, compute latent prediction error, and use thresholding or a lightweight classifier to detect glitch events.

## Baselines

Consider at least:

* frame difference
* feature distance
* mini_latent / lightweight latent dynamics proxy
* CNN-LSTM
* video autoencoder
* TimeSformer / VideoMAE small
* VLM-based detector only if feasible and properly bounded

## Strategic reason

This topic is strong because it connects:

* automated game testing,
* temporal video anomaly detection,
* world models,
* self-supervised representation learning,
* benchmark-based evaluation,
* and practical QA value for the game industry.
