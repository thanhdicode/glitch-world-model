\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related-work}

\paragraph{Video anomaly detection.}
Video anomaly detection commonly models normality or assigns anomaly scores to temporal segments.
Weakly supervised and memory-guided approaches illustrate the range of formulations used in
surveillance video \cite{sultani2018ucfcrime,park2020mnad}. Gameplay glitches differ in domain,
labels, and failure semantics, so those results are contextual rather than directly comparable.
Score-based out-of-distribution work similarly motivates cautious use of scalar anomaly scores
without implying calibrated probability or benchmark equivalence \cite{hendrycks2017baseline,
guo2017calibration}.

\paragraph{Gameplay glitches and automated testing.}
GlitchBench and TempGlitch make visual game failures more concrete as benchmark objects
\cite{glitchbench,tempglitch}. TempGlitch is particularly relevant because it emphasizes temporal
gameplay evidence, although the public artifact used here supplies binary video labels rather than
verified temporal spans. Broader game-testing research also stresses the continuing difficulty of
test generation, oracle design, and production integration \cite{politowski2022automatedtesting}.
Our study addresses only bounded video-level discrimination, not a complete automated QA system.

\paragraph{World models, JEPA, and latent prediction.}
World Models, PlaNet, and Dreamer establish a lineage of learned latent dynamics
\cite{worldmodels,planet,dreamerv3}. I-JEPA and V-JEPA motivate prediction in representation space
\cite{ijepa,vjepa}, while LeWorldModel provides the specific artifact-backed predictive model
family used in this repository \cite{lewm}. We evaluate latent prediction error as an anomaly
signal; we do not introduce a new architecture or claim control and planning performance.

\paragraph{Leakage-aware evaluation and reproducibility.}
Related sources can leak information when paired or derivative examples cross split boundaries,
inflating apparent generalization \cite{kaufman2011leakage,kapoor2023leakage}. Reproducibility
work further motivates explicit artifacts, commands, and reporting checklists
\cite{pineau2021reproducibility}. These concerns directly shape our role separation, same-support
comparisons, validators, and claim registry.
