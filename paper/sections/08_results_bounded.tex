\section{Bounded Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Main TempGlitch Pair-Disjoint Follow-up}

Table~\ref{tab:r5-bounded-results} reports the frozen pair-disjoint, non-locked, same-support
TempGlitch comparison. Every row uses 12 normal-negative and 22 buggy-positive evaluation
episodes after calibration on two normal episodes; source, pair, and episode overlap across roles
is zero.

\input{tables/r5_bounded_results}

The best recorded LeWM row is seed44 \texttt{lewm\_l2\_max} with mean episode aggregation. It
reaches AUROC 0.7159, AUPRC 0.8026, F1 0.7143, precision 0.7500, recall 0.6818, balanced accuracy
0.6326, and FPR@95TPR 0.7500. Its pair-bootstrap intervals are [0.5349, 0.8770] for AUROC and
[0.5854, 0.8293] for F1.

The best baseline by recorded AUROC is \texttt{feature\_distance} with
\texttt{top2\_mean} episode aggregation. It reaches AUROC 0.6136, AUPRC 0.7310, and F1 0.1600;
its AUROC interval is [0.4636, 0.7545]. Within this exact frozen non-locked split, the LeWM row
shows stronger observed same-support separation than the recorded simple baselines. The AUROC
intervals overlap, however, evaluation support is small, and the LeWM FPR@95TPR remains high.
The result therefore does not establish broad superiority.

\subsection{Secondary R5-XGame Comparison}

Table~\ref{tab:r5-xgame-results} reports the separate frozen non-locked R5-XGame family. On its
12-normal-negative and 60-buggy-positive evaluation support, the best recorded LeWM row reaches
AUROC 0.9097, compared with 0.7681 for feature distance and 0.7167 for frame difference. The
AUROC confidence intervals overlap, and the positive-heavy support limits the precision of
false-positive estimates.

\input{tables/r5_xgame_results}

These values are not directly compared with TempGlitch values. The two result families differ in
dataset distribution, class balance, calibration support, action mode, and training lineage. The
R5-XGame result is evidence about its own frozen split, not a test of cross-game generalization.
