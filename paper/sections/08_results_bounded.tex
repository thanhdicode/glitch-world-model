\section{Bounded Results}
\label{sec:results}

Table~\ref{tab:r5-bounded-results} reports the validator-backed pair-disjoint TempGlitch
follow-up. Every row uses exactly 12 normal-negative and 22 buggy-positive evaluation episodes;
the threshold is the normal-P95 value from two frozen calibration episodes. Cross-role
\texttt{source\_episode\_id}, \texttt{pair\_id}, and \texttt{source} overlap are all zero.

\input{tables/r5_bounded_results}

Within this frozen non-locked split, seed44 \texttt{lewm\_l2\_max} with mean episode aggregation
records AUROC 0.7159 and AUPRC 0.8026, compared with AUROC 0.6136 and AUPRC 0.7310 for the best
baseline AUROC row, \texttt{feature\_distance} with \texttt{top2\_mean}. The LeWM row's pair-bootstrap
AUROC interval is [0.5349, 0.8770], while the baseline interval is [0.4636, 0.7545]. These intervals
overlap, evaluation support is small, and the LeWM FPR@95TPR remains 0.7500.

The safe interpretation is therefore modest: within this exact same-support split, the best LeWM
configuration shows stronger observed separation than the simple baselines. The result does not
establish broad superiority, state of the art, temporal localization, or cross-game generalization.
Appendix~\ref{app:forbidden-claims} makes that distinction explicit.
