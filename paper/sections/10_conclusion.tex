\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This study establishes a leakage-aware, provenance-bound evaluation of latent-surprise signals for
gameplay glitch detection. On one frozen pair-disjoint non-locked TempGlitch split, the best
recorded LeWM configuration shows stronger observed same-support separation than the recorded
simple baselines. The result is accompanied by small support, wide and overlapping AUROC
intervals, and a high operating-point false-positive rate. A separate R5-XGame family offers
additional bounded evidence without serving as a cross-game comparison.

The evidence supports continued investigation of latent surprise, particularly with stronger
learned baselines, larger negative support, predeclared ablations, and datasets with verified
temporal annotations. It does not establish universal glitch detection, practical deployment
readiness, temporal localization, broad superiority, or locked-test performance. The immediate
next step is to execute Roadmap V4: harden the statistics and evaluation support locally, then add
learned-baseline, benchmark, and controlled-ablation evidence without relaxing the locked-test or
anti-overclaim boundaries.
