\section{Method Summary}
\label{sec:method}

The current manuscript is method-light by design. Its paper-facing method surface consists of
artifact-backed latent-surprise scoring from previously trained LeWM checkpoints together with two
simple comparison baselines, \texttt{frame\_diff} and train-normal-fitted
\texttt{feature\_distance}.

The LeWM family is conceptually aligned with joint-embedding predictive architectures
\cite{ijepa,vjepa,lewm} and with broader latent world-model work
\cite{worldmodels,planet,dreamerv3}. In the repository's bounded evaluation path, a trained
checkpoint maps input windows to latent prediction behavior from which scalar surprise statistics
can be derived. Different score variants and episode aggregations are compared within a frozen
manifest rather than through unconstrained post hoc tuning.

This section intentionally avoids claiming a new architecture contribution. For the current paper
state, the safe contribution is the evaluation framing and the artifact-backed reporting surface,
not a proof of broad method superiority. Any later expansion of the method section should remain
subordinate to the claim map and provenance appendices.
