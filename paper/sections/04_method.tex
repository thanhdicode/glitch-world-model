\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

\subsection{Normal-Only Latent Prediction}

The LeWM evidence used here comes from previously validated checkpoints trained under a
normal-only gameplay protocol. For an observation window, the encoder produces latent targets and
the predictor estimates future latent representations. A scalar surprise score is derived from
the discrepancy between predicted and target latents. The evaluated score registry includes
mean-, maximum-, and top-two summaries of mean-squared or L2 latent error. This study treats these
as recorded scoring variants, not as novel architectural contributions.

TempGlitch does not provide verified action traces for the current adapter, so its LeWM path uses
an explicitly documented zero-action input. The R5-XGame path uses its separately validated
real-action protocol. This difference is one reason the two evidence families are not directly
compared and cannot support an action-conditioning conclusion.

\subsection{Episode Aggregation}

Each window scorer is combined with mean, maximum, or top-two-mean episode aggregation. Mean
captures sustained elevation, whereas maximum and top-two-mean emphasize short high-surprise
segments. The main TempGlitch row uses mean episode aggregation. The manuscript reports recorded
variants without claiming that one aggregation is universally optimal.

\subsection{Simple Baselines}

\texttt{frame\_diff} measures visual change between adjacent frames and requires no learned fit.
\texttt{feature\_distance} fits its reference representation on allowed train-normal data only and
measures distance from that normal reference. Both baselines are scored and aggregated on the
same frozen evaluation support as LeWM within each evidence family.

\subsection{Normal-Only Threshold Calibration}

For every configuration, $\tau$ is the empirical 95th percentile of designated calibration-normal
episode scores. Buggy evaluation episodes are not used for fitting, configuration selection, or
threshold calibration. This is an operating-point rule, not neural probability calibration
\cite{guo2017calibration}. Locked test is neither materialized nor scored.
