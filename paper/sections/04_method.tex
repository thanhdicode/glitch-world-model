\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

\subsection{JEPA-Style Latent-Surprise Detector}

The evaluated LeWM path is a JEPA-style latent predictor trained on normal gameplay only. For a
window $w_{e,t}$ with context frames and optional actions, an encoder produces target latent
representations and a predictor estimates future latent states from the available context. Let
$z_{e,t}$ denote the target latent tensor and $\hat{z}_{e,t}$ the predicted tensor. A window-level
surprise signal is then derived from their discrepancy. The validated score registry includes
mean, maximum, and top-two-mean summaries of either elementwise mean-squared error or L2 error:
\begin{equation}
d_{e,t} \in \{\texttt{lewm\_mse\_mean},\texttt{lewm\_mse\_max},\texttt{lewm\_mse\_top2\_mean},
\texttt{lewm\_l2\_mean},\texttt{lewm\_l2\_max},\texttt{lewm\_l2\_top2\_mean}\}.
\end{equation}
We treat these as recorded detector variants exposed by validated artifacts rather than as new
architectural contributions.

TempGlitch does not provide verified action traces in the current adapter path, so its LeWM
evaluation uses an explicitly documented zero-action input. The separate R5-XGame family uses its
own real-action protocol. Because the evidence families differ in both domain and action mode,
their absolute scores are not used to claim action-conditioning benefit.

\subsection{Episode-Level Detector}

Window scores are aggregated to one episode score
\begin{equation}
S_e = A\left(d_{e,1}, \ldots, d_{e,T_e}\right),
\end{equation}
where $A$ is one of \texttt{mean}, \texttt{max}, or \texttt{top2\_mean}. Mean emphasizes
sustained elevated surprise, whereas \texttt{max} and \texttt{top2\_mean} emphasize short,
high-scoring segments. The paper reports the strongest recorded rows and their uncertainty without
claiming that any aggregation is universally optimal.

\subsection{Baseline Families}

Two simple baselines share the same episode-level reporting interface. \texttt{frame\_diff}
measures visual change between adjacent frames and requires no train-time fit.
\texttt{feature\_distance} fits a normal reference on allowed train-normal data only and scores
distance from that reference at evaluation time.

The exact-support K1 comparison adds three learned video baselines:
\texttt{video\_autoencoder}, \texttt{cnn\_lstm}, and \texttt{video\_transformer}. These models are
also fit only on allowed train-normal data and are aggregated with the same
\texttt{mean}/\texttt{max}/\texttt{top2\_mean} episode operators before comparison with LeWM.

\subsection{Qualitative Timelines and Mechanistic Readouts}

The detector also exposes window-level score series. These series are sufficient for qualitative
timeline figures but not for temporal localization metrics because the validated artifacts do not
contain true executable span annotations. Separately, the K3 ablation uses the same LeWM training
family to compare SIGReg-on/off and real-action/zero-action variants under a frozen
validation-normal objective. Those K3 rows are interpreted only through validation losses, not
through detection AUROC or F1.

\subsection{Normal-Only Threshold Calibration}

For every detection configuration, $\tau$ is the empirical 95th percentile of designated
calibration-normal episode scores. Buggy evaluation episodes are not used for fitting,
configuration selection, or threshold calibration. This is an operating-point rule, not neural
probability calibration \cite{guo2017calibration}. Locked test is neither materialized nor scored.
