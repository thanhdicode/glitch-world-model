\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

All empirical evidence is non-locked and validation-only. The main TempGlitch follow-up has 12
normal-negative and 22 buggy-positive evaluation episodes, while its operating threshold is
estimated from only two normal episodes. Its AUROC confidence intervals are wide, the best LeWM
and baseline intervals overlap, and the best LeWM FPR@95TPR is 0.7500. R5-XGame also has only 12
normal negatives, is positive-heavy with 60 buggy positives, and exhibits seed sensitivity.

TempGlitch provides binary video-level labels rather than verified temporal spans, while the
current executable GlitchBench path is image-level and the current validated WOB/XGame artifacts
carry episode-level roles only. Window scores therefore cannot be interpreted as validated glitch
localization. Qualitative surprise timelines may illustrate score dynamics, but they do not
measure onset, offset, duration, or event-level recall. The two evidence families are not
directly comparable because their support, calibration, domain, action mode, and training lineage
differ. R5-WOB has no normal-negative evaluation episodes and remains positive-probe evidence only.

The study includes simple frame-difference and feature-distance baselines but no learned video
baseline evaluated on the exact follow-up support. It contains no controlled evidence for SIGReg
benefit, action-conditioning benefit, broad cross-game generalization, real-time operation, or
deployment readiness. Locked test remains unmaterialized and unscored. Finally, the current
bounded package has not been compiled with the official Springer template because the required
local class and toolchain are unavailable; page fit and typesetting remain a submission-
preparation task.
