\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

All empirical evidence is non-locked and validation-only. The main TempGlitch follow-up has 12
normal-negative and 22 buggy-positive evaluation episodes, while its operating threshold is
estimated from only two normal episodes. Its AUROC confidence intervals are wide, the best LeWM
and baseline intervals overlap, and the best LeWM FPR@95TPR is 0.7500. R5-XGame also has only 12
normal negatives, is positive-heavy with 60 buggy positives, and exhibits seed sensitivity.

TempGlitch provides binary video-level labels rather than verified temporal spans, while the
current executable GlitchBench path is image-level and the current validated WOB/XGame artifacts
carry episode-level roles only. Window scores therefore cannot be interpreted as validated glitch
localization. Qualitative surprise timelines may illustrate score dynamics, but they do not
measure onset, offset, duration, or event-level recall.

K2 is a tiny synthetic-normal image-level split. Its negative comparison is informative, but it is
not the same task as episode-level gameplay QA on naturally occurring normal footage. K3 is a
mechanistic validation-normal training readout, not a binary glitch-detection benchmark. The two
TempGlitch and R5-XGame evidence families remain non-comparable because their support, calibration,
domain, action mode, and training lineage differ. R5-WOB has no normal-negative evaluation
episodes and remains positive-probe evidence only.

The study contains no locked-test result, no broad cross-game generalization result, no real-time
latency study, and no deployment trial. K3 also does not show a positive SIGReg or
action-conditioning readout for the specific validated artifact, so any broader claim in those
directions remains unsupported. Finally, the current manuscript package has not been compiled with
the official Springer template from this workspace because the required local class files and
toolchain are unavailable; page fit, bibliography warnings, and PDF metadata still require the
external official-kit build pass.

The current results also do not include a full TimeSformer or fully fine-tuned VideoMAE anomaly
detector. The included VideoMAE-small feature-distance row is a bounded learned baseline on the
current support, but larger video transformers remain future work under a matched compute and
selection protocol.
