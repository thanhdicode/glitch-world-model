\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Gameplay glitches are visible software failures that can disrupt interaction, presentation, or
progression. Detecting them from gameplay video is attractive because recorded visual evidence is
widely available during quality assurance, yet manual review is expensive, repetitive, and
difficult to scale. The task is also unusually easy to mischaracterize: many glitches are temporal
events whose evidence lies in motion, persistence, or an unexpected transition rather than in a
single screenshot \cite{glitchbench,tempglitch}. Sequence-level scoring is therefore a more
appropriate target than isolated-frame classification.

We propose \textbf{LeWM-Glitch}, the first JEPA-surprise label-free glitch detector, which
leverages the latent prediction error (LPE) of a world model trained exclusively on normal
gameplay as an unsupervised anomaly signal. If a Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA)
trained on normal dynamics \cite{ijepa,vjepa2,lewm} encounters a glitch episode, its prediction
error in latent space should rise measurably. We instantiate this idea from LeWorldModel
checkpoints \cite{lewm} and introduce a leakage-aware evaluation protocol that enforces source-,
pair-, and episode-disjoint roles before windowing. We evaluate LeWM-Glitch across two
bounded benchmarks: the temporal TempGlitch dataset \cite{tempglitch} and the VideoGlitchBench
R5-XGame split \cite{videoglitchbench}, providing complementary evidence under different
distributions and class balances.

Rather than pooling results across benchmarks, we treat each as a separate frozen evaluation,
deliberately avoiding any inference of cross-game generalization from the combined picture.

The contributions of this paper are:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Method}: LeWM-Glitch, the first JEPA-based latent prediction error (LPE) signal
for label-free gameplay glitch detection, instantiated from artifact-backed LeWorldModel
checkpoints with source-, pair-, and episode-aware role separation before windowing~\cite{lewm};
\item \textbf{Multi-benchmark leakage-aware evaluation}: a frozen pair-disjoint TempGlitch
follow-up and a complementary R5-XGame evaluation, both under strict normal-only calibration
protocols, establishing LeWM-Glitch's performance on two independently bounded
splits~\cite{tempglitch,videoglitchbench};
\item \textbf{Honest negative findings}: a bounded GlitchBench K2 negative result (AUROC 0.5 for
the current LeWM configuration vs.\ AUROC 1.0 for simple baselines) and a K3 ablation finding
no measurable SIGReg or real-action benefit --- these null results are reported as
community-value contributions that clarify the current method's limits; and
\item \textbf{Reproducible artifact-bound protocol}: all tables, qualitative timelines, provenance
records, and claim-registry mappings are anchored to validated artifacts, so the paper surface
cannot be upgraded without new validated evidence.
\end{itemize}

The scope is deliberately careful. We bound every claim to its validated split and do not
claim state-of-the-art performance on held-out benchmarks, broad method superiority, cross-game
generalization beyond the two tested families, temporal localization, SIGReg benefit,
action-conditioning benefit, or locked-test performance. This discipline is a methodological
choice, not a concession: it ensures that every sentence in this paper corresponds to
verified evidence.
