\section{Evaluation Protocol}
\label{sec:protocol}

The protocol is the core scientific contribution of the current scaffold. All train-dependent
fitting and threshold selection must respect split hygiene. In particular, train-dependent methods
fit on allowed train-normal data only, while the bounded R5 evaluation uses a frozen
identical-episode manifest with two calibration-normal episodes and a held evaluation pool of
normal and buggy episodes. This separation follows the general learn-predict and leakage-avoidance
concerns raised in prior work \cite{kaufman2011leakage,kapoor2023leakage}; the exact split
contract, however, is repository evidence rather than an external benchmark claim.

For the qualified R5 family, thresholds are derived from the $95^{\text{th}}$ percentile of the
two calibration-normal episode scores. Primary metrics are AUROC and AUPRC, while F1 at the
normal-calibrated threshold and FPR@95TPR are secondary. Grouped episode-bootstrap intervals are
used for uncertainty reporting in the current bounded table.

Calibration is used here in the narrow sense of selecting a threshold from designated
calibration-normal episodes. It should not be confused with neural probability calibration such as
temperature scaling \cite{guo2017calibration}.

Three protocol flags are especially important and remain fixed throughout the current paper phase:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \texttt{locked\_test\_materialized = false}
\item \texttt{locked\_test\_scored = false}
\item \texttt{validation\_buggy\_used\_for\_fit\_select = false}
\end{itemize}

These controls are repeated in the appendices because they define what the paper is allowed to say.
