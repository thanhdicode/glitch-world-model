\begin{abstract}
We propose \textbf{LeWM-Glitch}, a label-free gameplay glitch detector that exploits the latent
prediction error of a JEPA-style world model trained exclusively on normal gameplay.
Each video window is scored by the distance between the model's predicted and observed latent
states; window scores are aggregated to the episode level and thresholded against normal-only
calibration percentiles.
We instantiate LeWM-Glitch from artifact-backed LeWorldModel~\cite{lewm} checkpoints and evaluate
it under source-, pair-, and episode-disjoint role separation before windowing.

On a frozen pair-disjoint TempGlitch follow-up (12 normal-negative, 22 buggy-positive episodes),
the best LeWM-Glitch configuration achieves AUROC~0.716 (95\%~CI [0.535,\,0.877]); the strongest
simple baseline reaches AUROC~0.614 on the same support. The difference is numerically positive
but not statistically significant (DeLong $p{=}0.21$). An expanded TempGlitch run (29 normal,
38 buggy) yields AUROC~0.701, providing a robustness check on a larger support. On a secondary
frozen R5-XGame split (12 normal-negative, 60 buggy-positive episodes), LeWM-Glitch achieves
AUROC~0.910 (95\%~CI [0.828,\,0.972]) versus 0.768 for the best simple baseline.
We also report two bounded negative findings: LeWM-Glitch reaches only AUROC~0.50 on the
image-level GlitchBench K2 slice, and a mechanistic ablation (K3) finds no measurable benefit
from SIGReg regularization or real-action conditioning at this evaluation scale.
All evidence is non-locked and split-bounded; we make no claims of broad superiority,
cross-game generalization, temporal localization, or deployment readiness.
\keywords{gameplay glitch detection \and latent world models \and JEPA \and
latent prediction error \and leakage-aware evaluation \and video anomaly detection}
\end{abstract}
