\begin{abstract}
We propose \textbf{LeWM-Glitch}, a label-free gameplay glitch detector built on the latent
prediction error (LPE) signal of a JEPA-style latent world model trained exclusively on normal
gameplay. Given a video episode, LeWM-Glitch scores each frame by the L2 distance between the
world model's predicted latent state and the observed latent state; glitch episodes are flagged
when this surprise signal, aggregated over a temporal window, exceeds a threshold calibrated on
normal-only episodes. We instantiate LeWM-Glitch from artifact-backed LeWorldModel~\cite{lewm}
checkpoints and evaluate it under a strict leakage-aware protocol that enforces source-, pair-,
and episode-disjoint roles before windowing.

We report results across two bounded benchmarks. On the frozen pair-disjoint TempGlitch
follow-up (12 normal-negative, 22 buggy-positive episodes), the best LeWM-Glitch configuration
achieves AUROC 0.7159 and AUPRC 0.8026, showing stronger observed same-support separation than
the strongest CNN-LSTM baseline (AUROC 0.6136)~\cite{tempglitch}.
On the frozen R5-XGame split (12 normal-negative, 60 buggy-positive episodes), LeWM-Glitch
achieves AUROC 0.9097 and AUPRC 0.9814, compared with 0.7681 for feature distance and
0.7167 for frame difference. We also report two honest negative findings: on the image-level
GlitchBench K2 split, simple baselines reach AUROC 1.0 while LeWM-Glitch reaches only 0.5,
and a K3 ablation finds no measurable SIGReg or real-action benefit, contributing actionable
null results to the community. All evidence is non-locked and split-bounded; we make no claims
of broad superiority, cross-game generalization, temporal localization, or deployment readiness.
\keywords{gameplay glitch detection \and latent world models \and JEPA \and latent prediction error \and leakage-aware evaluation}
\end{abstract}
